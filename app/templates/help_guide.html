Your Wishlist

Adding Items

  1. Click "My List" in the navigation bar
  2. Click the "Add Item" button
  3. Fill out the item information:
    • Item Title (required) - Name of the item
    • Description - Be specific! Include size, color, model number, or any preferences
      Example: "Size Medium, Navy Blue, Model XR-500"
    • Link/URL - Where to buy it (Amazon, store website, etc.)
    • Price - Helps gift-givers budget
    • Allow multiple people to buy this - Check this for items like gift cards where multiple gifts are okay
  4. Click "Save Item"

Received Items (Archive)

Why Use the Archive?

After birthdays and holidays, you'll receive gifts from your wishlist. Marking them as "received" keeps your active wishlist clean and current while maintaining a history of what you've gotten.

Marking Items as Received

  1. Go to "My List"
  2. Make sure you're on the "Active Items" tab
  3. Find the item you received
  4. Click the green checkmark button (‚úì)
  5. The item moves to your "Received Items" tab
When to do this: After Christmas, birthdays, or any time you receive a gift from your list!

Viewing Received Items

  1. Go to "My List"
  2. Click the "Received Items" tab
  3. You'll see all items you've marked as received with the date

What you'll see:

  • Item title and description
  • When you received it (e.g., "Received: Dec 25, 2024")
  • Price and link (if you added them)

Restoring Items to Active List

Want the same item again (different size, color, or just want another)? You can restore it:

  1. Go to "My List" ‚Üí "Received Items" tab
  2. Find the item you want again
  3. Click "Restore"
  4. You'll see the edit form with a yellow warning:
    ‚ö†Ô∏è Restoring Archived Item
    This item was marked as received on [date]. Please verify the URL and price are still current before saving.
  5. Update any details (description, URL, price, etc.)
  6. Click "Save Item"
  7. A NEW COPY appears on your Active Items list
  8. The original stays in Received Items (history preserved!)
Example: You got a blue sweater (size M) for Christmas. Mark it received. Next year you want another in size L. Restore the item, change the description to "size L, navy color," save. Now you have a new active item and the history of what you got last year!

Deleting from Archive

Want to permanently remove an item from your archive?

  1. Go to "My List" ‚Üí "Received Items" tab
  2. Click the trash icon (üóëÔ∏è)
  3. Confirm deletion

Managing Children's Lists

When to Create a Child Profile

Create a child profile only for young children who can't manage their own wishlist yet (typically under 10-12 years old).

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Warnings
  • Coordinate with your spouse! Only ONE parent should create the child's profile
  • Don't create for older children/adults - They should have their own account (text Paul for an invitation)
  • Check first! Your spouse may have already created it

Creating a Child Profile

  1. Go to "My List"
  2. Scroll to the bottom
  3. Click "Add Child Profile"
  4. Read the confirmation warning carefully
  5. Click "Yes, Create Profile"
  6. Enter your child's name
  7. Click "Add Child"

Managing Your Child's List

Once created, you'll see your child's list in the "Children's Lists You Manage" section.

To add/edit items for your child:

  1. Go to "My List"
  2. Scroll to "Children's Lists You Manage"
  3. Click on your child's name
  4. Add, edit, organize, and manage items just like your own list
  5. Click "‚Üê Back to My List" to return to your list

Everything works the same:

  • Add items with descriptions and links
  • Organize by priority
  • Mark items as received after holidays
  • Restore items from archive

What About the Other Parent?

Both parents can view the child's list when shopping for family, but only the parent who created it can manage (add/edit/delete items). This prevents duplicate profiles and confusion.

When to Graduate to Their Own Account

When your child is old enough to manage their own wishlist (typically 10-12+), they can get their own account. Text Paul to send them an invitation!

Shopping for Others

Finding Family Members' Lists

  1. Click "Dashboard" in the navigation bar
  2. You'll see all family members
  3. Click on anyone's name to view their wishlist

Understanding What You See

When viewing someone's list:

  • Priority order - Items at the top are most wanted
  • Active items only - You won't see items they've already received
  • Full details - Description, link to buy, price, any notes
  • Claim status - See if items are available or already claimed
üí° Pro Tip: If someone has prioritized their list well, start at the top for the gifts they want most!

Claiming Items

When you decide to buy something for someone:

  1. View their wishlist
  2. Find an item you want to buy
  3. Click the "Claim" button
  4. The button changes to "Unclaim" (green checkmark)

What claiming does:

  • ‚úÖ Tells other family members this item is covered
  • ‚úÖ Prevents duplicate gifts
  • ‚ùå The list owner CANNOT see what's claimed
  • ‚ùå The list owner CANNOT see WHO claimed it

Unclaiming Items

Changed your mind? No problem!

  1. View the person's wishlist
  2. Find the item you claimed
  3. Click "Unclaim"
  4. The item becomes available again for others

Filtering Available Items

Want to see only items that haven't been claimed yet?

  1. View someone's wishlist
  2. Toggle "Show only available items" at the top
  3. Claimed items are hidden

Multiple People Buying the Same Item

Some items allow multiple claims (like gift cards or consumables):

  • These items show "Multiple allowed" or "Up to X people"
  • You can claim even if others already have
  • Great for things like movie tickets, restaurant gift cards, etc.

Privacy Reminder

ü§´ Keep It Secret!
  • Don't tell people what you claimed for them
  • Don't discuss who's buying what in family chats
  • The whole point is to maintain surprise!

Account Settings

Changing Your Password

  1. Click your name in the top right
  2. Select "Settings"
  3. Enter your current password
  4. Enter your new password twice
  5. Click "Change Password"
‚úì Password changed! Use your new password next time you log in.

Forgot Your Password?

If you're locked out:

  1. Go to the login page
  2. Click "Forgot Password?"
  3. Enter your email address
  4. Check your email for a reset link
  5. Click the link and set a new password
What if the link expired?

Password reset links expire after 1 hour. If yours expired, just request a new one - it only takes a minute!

Updating Your Name

  1. Click your name in the top right
  2. Select "Settings"
  3. Enter your new display name
  4. Click "Save"

Why your name matters: Your name appears on your wishlist and throughout the site. Choose something family members will recognize!

Tips & Best Practices

Creating a Great Wishlist

‚úì DO
  • Be specific about size, color, model
  • Include links to where to buy
  • Add price ranges
  • Use priority ordering
  • Keep it updated year-round
  • Mark items as received after holidays
‚úó DON'T
  • Be vague ("a sweater")
  • Leave out important details
  • Add only during holidays
  • Let old items pile up
  • Forget to update if your preferences change
  • Tell people what you claimed

After the Holidays

  1. Mark items as received - Clean up your active list
  2. Delete items you no longer want - Keep your list current
  3. Add new ideas - Start building for next year
  4. Thank your gift-givers! - Even though they don't know you knew üòä

Keeping Lists Updated

Great wishlists are living documents:

  • Add items whenever you think of something
  • Remove things if you buy them yourself
  • Update preferences if they change
  • Adjust priority as your wants change

Privacy Etiquette

Golden Rule: Don't discuss who's buying what!
  • Keep your claims to yourself
  • Don't ask others what they claimed
  • Don't coordinate "I'll get this, you get that" publicly
  • The surprise is part of the fun!
